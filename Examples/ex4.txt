# Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Consider the following:

A\vec{v} = \lambda\vec{v}

since

A^{-1}A \vec{v} = A^{-1}\lambda \vec{v}
\frac{1}{\lambda}

This means 1/@L is an eigenvalue of AINV

## Remark

if we know @L, then the eigenspace

E_@L

is equal to 

null(A-@lI)

## Example

A = \begin{bmatrix}
8 & 6 \\
1 & 7 \\
\end{bmatrix}

A = [
8 6
1 7
]

det(A-@l) = det([
8-@l 6
1 7-@l
])
